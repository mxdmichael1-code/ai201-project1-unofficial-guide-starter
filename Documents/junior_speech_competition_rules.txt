JUNIOR_SPEECH_COMPETITION_RULES
Document type: Judge training material
Format: WSDA Junior Speech
Primary users: Junior speech judges
Purpose: Explain the main competition rules for Junior Speech, including time limit, originality, cue cards, and props.

SECTION 1: DOCUMENT PURPOSE

This document explains the basic rules judges should know when evaluating Junior Speech.

Judges should use these rules to understand what is allowed during the speech and what should or should not affect scoring.

SECTION 2: SPEECH TIME LIMIT

The time limit for Junior Speech is 3 minutes.

A speaker may give a speech shorter than 3 minutes.

A speech shorter than 3 minutes should not be penalized only because it is shorter.

When the speech reaches 3 minutes, the speaker must stop immediately.

Judges should not require a minimum speech length.

Judges should evaluate whether the speech is effective within the time used.

SECTION 3: ORIGINALITY RULE

The speech does not have to be original.

A speaker may write an original speech.

A speaker may also revise, add to, excerpt from, or directly use an existing speech written by someone else.

Judges should not use originality as a scoring standard in Junior Speech.

Judges should not penalize a speaker simply because the speech content is adapted from or based on another existing text.

SECTION 4: CUE CARD RULE

Speakers are encouraged to deliver the speech from memory.

However, speakers are allowed to use cue cards during the speech.

Whether the speaker is fully memorized should not be treated as an independent scoring standard.

A speaker should not automatically receive a lower score only because they used cue cards.

However, judges may consider the actual effect of cue card use on performance.

Cue cards may reduce eye contact with the judge and audience.

Cue cards may affect fluency, confidence, audience connection, and the final presentation effect.

Judges should evaluate how the speech is delivered, not simply whether cue cards were used.

SECTION 5: PROP RULE

Speakers may not use self-provided props during the speech, except for cue cards.

Speakers may not use PowerPoint slides.

Speakers may not use assistive props.

Speakers may not use other presentation tools that support or replace the speech.

Junior Speech should be evaluated based on the speaker's speech content and speech delivery.

If a speaker uses a prohibited prop or tool, the judge should follow tournament procedure or report the issue to tournament staff.

SECTION 6: WHAT JUDGES SHOULD REMEMBER

The speech time limit is 3 minutes.

There is no minimum speech time.

A shorter speech should not be penalized only for being short.

The speaker must stop when 3 minutes is reached.

The speech does not need to be original.

Originality is not a scoring standard.

Cue cards are allowed.

Memorization is encouraged but not required.

Cue card use may affect the final presentation if it reduces eye contact, fluency, or audience connection.

Props are not allowed, except for cue cards.

PowerPoint and assistive props are not allowed.

ORIGINAL_ORATORY_FORMAT_AND_ROUND_PROCEDURE
Document type: Judge training material
Format: Original Oratory (OO)
Primary users: Speech judges
Purpose: Explain the basic nature of Original Oratory and the round procedure, following the official event description.

SECTION 1: WHAT ORIGINAL ORATORY IS

Original Oratory is a speech event in which the speaker chooses a meaningful topic and develops an original speech.

The speaker should collect relevant materials and information, use personal thinking and judgment, and present the topic in an interesting and useful way.

The goal is for the audience to benefit from the speech. A strong speech should help the audience understand, reflect on, or care about the topic.

SECTION 2: TOPIC AND APPROACH

Many Original Oratory speeches focus on an existing problem and propose possible solutions.

However, a problem-solution structure is not the only acceptable approach.

A speaker may warn the audience about a hidden or potential threat.

A speaker may explain the purpose or value of participating in a project.

A speaker may praise or reflect on a person.

A speaker has freedom in choosing the topic and materials.

Judges should not require every Original Oratory speech to follow the same structure. Judges should evaluate whether the speech text is effective and whether the delivery is effective.

SECTION 3: ORIGINALITY REQUIREMENT

The speech should show originality from the speaker.

The speaker may use research and outside materials, but the final speech should be the speaker's own work.

A speaker may not directly quote more than 150 Chinese characters from another person's speech or written script.

Broadly paraphrasing another person's speech is also prohibited.

Judges should understand that research is allowed, but copying another speech or broadly rewriting another speech is not allowed.

If any speech content raises plagerism/copyright/extreme opinion concern for the judges, the judge should raise it to the tabroom.

SECTION 4: WHAT JUDGES SHOULD EVALUATE

Judges should evaluate Original Oratory based on the effectiveness of the text and the effectiveness of the delivery.

Text effectiveness includes whether the topic is meaningful, whether the speech has a clear purpose, whether the ideas are developed, and whether the audience can benefit from the speech.

Delivery effectiveness includes whether the speaker communicates clearly, keeps the audience engaged, and presents the speech effectively.

Judges should evaluate the speech performed in the room, not the judge's personal agreement or disagreement with the topic.

SECTION 5: PRELIMINARY ROUND GROUPING

In preliminary rounds, all competitors are randomly assigned to different groups for each round.

Each group usually has 5 to 8 competitors.

Competitors may be assigned to different groups in different rounds.

Judges should evaluate the speakers in their assigned group.

SECTION 6: GROUP AND SPEAKING ORDER

Competitors may use their competitor number to check the group assignment and speaking order for each round.

Competitors should speak according to the assigned speaking order.

Before beginning the speech, each competitor should introduce:
1. competitor number;
2. Chinese name;
3. speech title.

Judges should record the correct competitor number, name, speech title, score, and rank.

SECTION 7: SPEECH TIME LIMITS IN ROUND PROCEDURE

Junior division speakers have a maximum speech time of 5 minutes and 30 seconds.

Senior division speakers have a maximum speech time of 10 minutes and 30 seconds.

Judges should know which division they are judging and should follow the tournament timing rules.

SECTION 8: DURING THE SPEECH

Judges should listen carefully and take notes during the speech.

Judges should not interrupt the speaker unless there is a serious safety, procedural, or tournament rule issue.

Judges should evaluate both the content of the speech and the presentation of the speech.

Judges should not provide oral comments throughout the round.

SECTION 9: AFTER THE SPEECH

After finishing the speech, the competitor should listen to the other competitors in the same group.

A competitor should not leave immediately after giving the speech.

All competitors may leave only after all speeches in the group have finished and the judge gives permission or tournament procedure allows dismissal.

Judges should remind competitors to remain in the room if they try to leave early.


SECTION 10: QUICK REFERENCE

Original Oratory is an individual speech event.

The speaker chooses a meaningful topic.

A problem-solution format is common but not required.

The speech should be original.

Direct quotation from another speech or script may not exceed 150 Chinese characters.

Broad paraphrasing of another person's speech is prohibited.

Preliminary groups usually include 5 to 8 competitors.

The competitor introduces competitor number, Chinese name, and speech title before speaking.

Junior division speech time limit is 5 minutes and 30 seconds.

Senior division speech time limit is 10 minutes and 30 seconds.

The competitor must listen to the rest of the group after speaking.

Judges evaluate text effectiveness and delivery effectiveness.
